The girl in her vain dream.
Young teenage girl Kelden is aware of what grownups are aware of. She was raised by her widowed mother in a tiny village. She lost her father at a crucial time in her life, and after his death, her mother remarried a guy who lived day and night with a bottle of whiskey. She has a younger brother, whom she views as more fortunate than she is. She usually spent her time herding cattle, wandering the forest with them to graze, and gathering firewood for the chilly winter nights. Even yet, her sober stepfather is still making a fuss and threatening the entire family while throwing stuff at her mother, who is an innocent victim. It is tough for her to get up early in the morning and start her march to high school while carrying a packed lunch across her back because she has to travel miles to go to the school where she studies. Nevertheless, she continues to attend boarding school even when other kids her age are having fun and receiving the best parental guidance. For Kelden, she is consistently ethically responsible and thoughtful, enabling her to manage such a demanding reality while holding a strong urge to learn and escape the ocean of misery. Kelden's motivation to study is boosted by sentiments of her family and how her stepfather devastates them; despite everything, she consistently achieves the highest grade in the class on her yearly exam. The school had nothing but praise for a little girl who is ambitious to become a doctor because of her good behavior and performance. Moreover she turns into a model student at the school. For Kelden, living in a hostel is the only temple that is truly secure for her to learn and conquer adversities. She always worries about leaving on vacation, despite how much she misses her mother and brother, because of her clumsy stepfather, who yelled at her with his bulging eyeball almost poking out. Kelden, however, acted as though nothing had occurred and joyfully returned to her village. The excitement was again dashed when she noticed her mother's facial scars and sensed the bad atmosphere coming from her house. She can do nothing but sit in a dark, gloomy corner of her store and cry. She is reminded of her earlier days when she used to chase after cattle through bushes that were both thick and thin. She gets up at the crack of dawn to perform her morning household duties, which include preparing the cattle's feast and pouring water into the altar. Her morning routine includes eating breakfast early and running after cattle to ensure that they are returned safely and with enough time to graze in the lush green meadow.Her only neighbor and childhood friend in her village is Pema Zangmo. Given the fact that they are both the same age and in the same grade, she was raised in a comfortable home and attends an urban school. Kelden has always envied her for coming from a wealthy family and having a supporting family. She took her cattle to the jungle to graze one nice day as she usually did, but this time she ventured a little more from her usual pasture area. She carried a rope to gather firewood and a meal she had packed. Since Kelden is a fearless little girl, walking alone into the woods is nothing out of the ordinary for her. She is well-versed in wild flora and fauna, thus she could quickly get away from troublesome situations. Kelden was exceptionally uneasy and unaccustomed to the deep forest on that particular day nevertheless. The area is covered in throne chestnut and big, broad oak trees. With no signs of any nearby people, the area seems mysterious and haunted. She continued to be brave enough to herd cattle and gather more firewood. She lost one of her cows, which produced the most milk and was her primary source of income at home, all of a sudden. When she was alarmed, she chased the other cattle in her herd home before going back to look for a missing cow. She kept looking for the cow as the sun sank over the crest of the young mountain while drenched in perspiration and gripped by the anxiety that made her the most tense.

Kelden heard a faint mooing in the distance as she looked for her missing cow. She spotted her cow lying on the ground helpless in a tiny clearing after following the noises. She soon noticed that the cow was hurt and required immediate medical care. Without a second thought, Kelden looked at the cow's wounds and noticed a broken leg. She used twigs and branches she had collected from the forest to make a splint for the cow's leg, and then she tied the splint to the leg with her rope. She discovered that she had become lost in the jungle when she got up to take the cow back home. She didn't know where she was and had no way to get back. Kelden realized she needed to maintain composure and think logically. She waited for darkness to fall and then searched for the North Star, remembering that her mother had taught her how to navigate by the stars. Once she did, she realized she needed to go the other way in order to return home. They faced numerous obstacles as Kelden and her cow set out on their journey through the forest. It was challenging to move quickly due to the dense foliage, and it was hot and humid, making it challenging to stay cool. In order to avoid getting lost in the thick woods, they had to carefully navigate through rugged terrain and deep ravines. Kelden was fully aware that one false move could mean the end for both her and her cow, and that each step they took seemed to take them more into the unknown. As Kelden and her cow ventured further into the forest, they came across a large array of wild animals. They had to remain cautious around potentially harmful animals including snakes, boars and big cats. To find suitable places to rest and to find food and water, Kelden had to use her knowledge of the jungle. However, not all of the life in the jungle was friendly. In order to escape dangerous predators, Kelden and her cow had to move continuously and be extra diligent. One night, while Kelden and her cow were sleeping, a pack of wolves ambushed them. The howls of the wolves Kelden heard in the distance were getting closer and closer. She gathered twigs and branches to make a fire to stave off the wolves since she knew she had to act quickly to save her cow. She used her rope to tie her cow to a nearby tree, and then stood guard with a makeshift spear she had fashioned from a stick and a sharp rock. Kelden could feel her heart thumping in her chest as the wolves drew nearer. She was prepared for it since she grasped that this was a battle for her life. With their eyes gleaming in the firelight, the wolves were surrounding her and moving closer and closer. In preparation for the attack, Kelden held her ground. The first wolf lunged at her, and Kelden thrust her spear forward, impaling the wolf on the sharp rock. The other wolves hesitated for a moment, but then they too began to attack. Kelden fought bravely, wielding her spear with skill and precision, but there were too many of them. She was outnumbered and outmatched. Just as the wolves were closing in for the kill, Kelden heard a loud roar in the distance. She couldn't believe her eyes…….

Just as the wolves were closing in for the kill, Kelden heard a loud roar in the distance. She couldn't believe her eye. A blurry, enormous, fuzzy beast that looked like a chimpanzee approached Kelden, who was scared and stood idle. The wolves that rounded Kelden abruptly bowed their heads, screamed in a sob-like tone, and fled silently. Even though Kelden was quite glad to get rid of the wolves, she was also more terrified this time. With each step closer to her, the sound of the animals' feet intensifies until it resembles that of a huge elephant. She is still numb and has nothing to do. She bravely defended her illed cow from the attack. She could see the animal approaching and recognized it as a female bear roaring angrily as though she had lost her cub. She was silently wondering if it would be their last destination as she glared at her cow in despair. Kelden, however, could not help but think of her grandmother's tale of escaping a bear. She remembered clearly what her grandmother had said: "Whenever we meet a bear in the jungle, avoid running uphills because they have strong grips to climb up and could easily get caught by them." Instead, try running downhill, which will be simpler and make it harder for the bear to control their speed and cause them to land up falling after a speedy run. After that, she remembered everything, mentally counted to three, gripped the cow's rope tightly, and began sprinting 100 meters across the forest. Despite the challenges, she was keen about saving herself and the cow. The bear that was after them was moving quickly, howling loudest she could, chasing the quiet sleeping birds and rattling the homes of squirrels. A few meters down, Kelden changed her direction and stealthily hid the cow and herself behind a massive rock. She acknowledges her grandmother's advice and keeps a silent watch on the bear. Fortunately, the bear lost control of its speed and rolled down to a mile distance. She hugged her cow after exhaling deeply for a long time. Her mother was anxiously awaiting her daughter's arrival back home. She performs a simple ritual and asks the local astrologer to predict Kelden's safe return. The nasty stepfather, on the other hand, was fiercely accusing Kelden for the death of the cow while holding his booze bottle in his hand. He was yelling at her wife for her daughter's failings at home and acting in an aggressive manner. Kelden's stomach grumbled from two days of starvation. She quickly finds her way back home by using the stars as a guide. Fortunately, after a number of struggles, Kelden managed to locate a portion of her route and hastily returned home. She couldn't believe her stepfather was waiting for her in an unpleasant manner, though....
